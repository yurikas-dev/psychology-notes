## What Is Sport & Exercise Psychology?

Sport and exercise psychology is the scientific study of how **psychological factors affect performance** in sport and physical activity — and conversely, how participation in sport and exercise affects psychological development and well-being.

It bridges two questions:

> *How does the mind influence athletic performance?*
> *How does physical activity influence the mind?*

## Two Sides of the Field

### Performance Enhancement
This side focuses on helping athletes and performers reach their potential. It examines mental factors such as motivation, concentration, confidence, and the ability to manage pressure.

### Exercise and Well-being
This side explores how physical activity affects mental health — including its role in reducing anxiety, depression, and stress, and its contribution to overall quality of life.

## Key Practitioners

### Sport Psychologist
A licensed psychologist with specialized training in sport and performance. Qualified to diagnose and treat clinical mental health conditions in addition to performance work.

### Mental Performance Consultant (MPC)
A practitioner focused specifically on psychological skills training for performance — without necessarily holding a clinical license. Works with athletes, coaches, and teams to develop mental skills.

Most applied work in sport settings is done by MPCs rather than clinical sport psychologists.

## What Mental Skills Are Trained?

Sport and exercise psychologists work with a range of mental skills, including:

- **Goal setting** — structuring objectives to drive motivation and focus
- **Imagery and visualization** — mentally rehearsing performance
- **Self-talk** — managing internal dialogue under pressure
- **Arousal regulation** — controlling anxiety and activation levels
- **Concentration and focus** — maintaining attention during competition
- **Confidence building** — developing belief in one's abilities
- **Resilience** — recovering from setbacks and mistakes

## Beyond Sport: Transferable Skills

A key insight of this field is that psychological skills are not sport-specific. The same mental tools that help an athlete perform under pressure apply directly to:

| Sport Context | Everyday Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Pre-competition routine | Preparing for a job interview or exam |
| Managing performance anxiety | Public speaking or presentations |
| Focusing under pressure | Deadlines and high-stakes decisions |
| Recovering from errors | Dealing with failure or setbacks |
| Team cohesion | Workplace collaboration |

This transferability is central to the value of studying performance psychology — the skills are broadly applicable to any domain requiring consistent, high-level performance.

## Why Study It?

Mental factors are widely recognized as a critical determinant of performance at all levels. Physical talent and technical skill are necessary but not sufficient — athletes who cannot manage their psychology under pressure rarely reach their potential.

As the field has grown, so has its application beyond elite sport, reaching recreational athletes, performing artists, military personnel, surgeons, and business professionals.
